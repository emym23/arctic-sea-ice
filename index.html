What is Thermodynamics and Why is it Useful Here? [EDIT]

Thermodynamics is the process that affects the freezing and the melting of sea ice. For example, when the temperature of the ocean reaches the freezing point for salt water (which would be -1.8 degrees for Celsius and 28.8 degrees for Fahrenheit), the ice begins to grow and when the temperature rises above the freezing point, the ice begins to melt. The amount and the rates for the growth and melting of the ice depend on how the heat is exchanged within the sea ice itself and between the top and bottom of the ice. Since we're tracking the growth and melting of sea ice, we are essectially tracking the thermodynaics of the sea ice.
