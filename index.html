Introduction

First of all, in order to understand the basis of our project we have to talk about Thermodynaics. Thermodynamics is the process that affects the freezing and the melting of sea ice. For example, when the temperature of the ocean reaches the freezing point for salt water (which would be -1.8 degrees for Celsius and 28.8 degrees for Fahrenheit), the ice begins to grow and when the temperature rises above the freezing point, the ice begins to melt. The amount and the rates for the growth and melting of the ice depend on how the heat is exchanged within the sea ice itself and between the top and bottom of the ice. Since we're tracking the growth and melting of sea ice, we are essectially tracking the thermodynaics of the sea ice.

In 2019 the maximum was at around 2.072 months, where the sea ice extent reached 14.5544 million square kilometers. Then the minimum occurred during the end of the eight month, when x ≅ 8.889 months and the sea ice extent was about 4.4345 million square kilometers.
